I Love Cooking

(My Passion for cooking makes me happy)

I've always enjoyed cooking and experimenting with new ingredients and techniques to make great and enjoyable meals. I began cooking with my mother when I was approximately eight years old. My mother taught me to cook. It is always enjoyable and exciting to be in the kitchen, preparing satisfying meals. Because I have children, I prepare meals at home every day. You can use your creativity to create your own recipes as you cook. You are free to pick your own ingredients and how you want to cook them.

I've always tried cooking various foods like Nepalese, Indian, Chinese, American, and Italian cuisines, all of which have a wonderful and relaxing flavor. Since I learned these skills from my mother, I also want to pass them on to my children. As a result, I ask them to help me when I cook. For example, I ask my son to roll the dough while I make roti, crack the eggs when I make omelets, spread the cheese and vegetables on the pizza, and fill and wrap the dumplings when I make dumplings. He also likes helping in the kitchen.

Why I love Cooking

  • Food prepared at home is always healthy and nutritious.
  • Cooking at home brings families closer.
  • We can use fresh produce in all home-cooked meals while keeping calories low.
  • Making food for your family can help you feel satisfied and happy within as you can ensure that you and your family eat fresh meals.
  • Cooking can also help us become more creative.
  • The ability to cook can help you save money and enhance both your physical and emotional health

So far, I've made a variety of foods, but momo, commonly known as dumplings, is one of my all-time favorite Nepali dishes because it is packed with healthy veggies like onions, cabbage, cilantro, spring onions, garlic, etc. Dumplings can be made vegetarian, with chicken, or with any kind of meat filling. They are usually made during the rainy or winter season, as they can give warmth to the body when served with chicken soup. Below is the recipe for preparing delicious chicken momos.

Chicken Momo Recipe

Ingredients

For Dough
  • 2 cups All Purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp salt
For Filling
  • 1 cup cabbage grated.
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • ½ cup Onion finely chopped
  • 1 tsp garlic chopped or crushed.
  • ½ cup finely chopped spring onions
  • 1 tsp soya sauce
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp meat masala
  • ½ cup finely chopped cilantro
  • 2 lb. ground chicken or meat of your choice

Directions to make Chicken Momo

  1. Mix all-purpose flour, salt, and knead to a stiff dough with water.
  2. In a big bowl add all the vegetables and ground chicken, salt to taste, soya sauce, all spices and butter and mix until everything looks nice and juicy.
  3. Roll the dough thin and round with size 4”- 5.”
  4. Make the edges of rolled dough wet and place some filling in the center. Make sure not to put too much filling which may be hard to wrap.
  5. Bring the edges together twisting to cover the filling. You can give your own shape and finish wrapping all momos.
  6. Apply little oil to base of steamer and place all wrapped momos in steamer.
  7. Steam those momos in for about 20 mins and serve with some tomato chutney and chicken soup(optional).
